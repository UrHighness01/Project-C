# Summary of Tao's 2016 Paper: Global Regularity of the Navier-Stokes Equation in the Absence of Boundaries

## Authors
Terence Tao

## Publication
Journal of the American Mathematical Society, Vol. 29, No. 2 (2017), pp. 283-311
arXiv:1510.03774 (submitted Oct 2015, published 2016)

## Main Result
The paper proves global regularity for the incompressible Navier-Stokes equations in \(\mathbb{R}^3\) when the initial data is sufficiently small in the critical Besov space \(F^{-1}_{\infty,\infty}(\mathbb{R}^3)\).

## Key Concepts
- **Critical Spaces**: The critical Sobolev space for NS is \(H^{1/2}\), and the critical Besov space \(F^{-1}_{\infty,\infty}\) is related to it.
- **Small Data**: For initial data with norm smaller than some threshold, global smooth solutions exist.

## Proof Strategy
1. Assume a blow-up occurs at finite time T.
2. Use scaling and dimensional analysis to analyze the behavior near the blow-up time.
3. Employ Besov space techniques and vorticity estimates.
4. Derive a contradiction by showing that the solution cannot blow up in finite time for small initial data.

## Implications
- Advances our understanding of the Navier-Stokes regularity problem.
- Shows that smoothness breakdown, if it occurs, must happen for large initial data.
- Provides insight into the mechanism of potential blow-up via vorticity concentration.

## Relation to Millennium Prize
This is a partial result that rules out blow-up for small data in critical spaces, but does not solve the full problem of whether smooth solutions exist globally for arbitrary smooth initial data.
